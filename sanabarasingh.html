Sanabarasingh — A Legacy of Precision and Pattern

Beautifully vibrant and deeply rooted in tradition, Sanabarasingh Ikat captures the essence of Odisha’s heritage through symbolic motifs and expert craftsmanship.

About the Cluster:

Sanabarasingh is a renowned village in Odisha, celebrated for its master weavers who have safeguarded traditional Ikat weaving techniques for generations. Located in the serene district of Cuttack, this village thrives amidst culture, where weaving is a way of life. Many families in this closely-knit community are deeply engaged in handcrafted production, continuing a legacy that’s both personal and cultural.

Cultural Significance:

Each piece woven in Sanabarasingh tells a story — of devotion, discipline, and deep-rooted cultural knowledge passed over generations. These intricate designs go beyond mere ornamentation; they serve as visual narrators of spiritual beliefs, ancestral vision, and collective identity. Many of the motifs draw inspiration from local folklore, sacred rituals, and age-old symbols that carry deep meaning for the community. Every fabric, therefore, becomes more than just woven material — it is a living tapestry of tradition, emotion, and cultural memory.

About the Craft:

The weaving process in Sanabarasingh is highly meticulous. Artisans here are known for double knot — a technique where both warp and weft threads are tie-dyed with great care before the dyeing process. The resulting patterns include:

  • Scared symbols
  • Floral elements
  • Geometric forms
    • Each design is executed with astonishing precision, reflecting the artisan's deep understanding of the craft.
