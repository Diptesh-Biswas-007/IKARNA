Maniabandha — Where Art Meets Spirituality

A village where threads speak stories of faith and finesse, Maniabandha’s khadi is a jewel in Odisha's rich textile heritage, known for intricate patterns and a deeply spiritual soul.

About the Cluster:

Maniabandha, located in the Cuttack district of Odisha, is widely celebrated for its Buddhist weaving community and their remarkable handmade craftsmanship. The name itself means "jewel of weaving," which perfectly captures the pride and tradition of the village.

Cultural Significance:

Maniabandha is not just a weaving hub — it's a center where craft and Buddhism coexist. Weaving here is a form of spiritual practice, guided by:

  • Mindfulness
  • Cultural rituals
  • Discipline passed down generations
The village's Buddhist festivals also serve as stages for displaying the weavers' exceptional textiles.

About the Craft:

The weavers in Maniabandha are known for mastering the double ikat technique, where both warp and weft threads are tie-dyed with precise planning before weaving. Each khadi saree is:

  • Handcrafted from start to finish
  • Mathematically intricate
  • Supplemented in design
Motifs frequently include:
  • Floral patterns
  • Birds and animals
  • Mythological scenes (e.g., Ramayana, Mahabharata)
The artisans primarily use natural dyes, resulting in vibrant, earthy colours that enhance the fabric’s charm.
